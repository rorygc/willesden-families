Family-friendly restaurants in North West London

Kids' menus, high chairs, and places where nobody minds the mess.

Ten tried-and-tested restaurants across Queen's Park, West Hampstead, Kilburn, Cricklewood, and Hampstead — all with proper kids' menus, colouring sheets, and the kind of relaxed atmosphere where a dropped fork doesn't cause a crisis.

Queen's Park

Giggling Squid

all ages££Queen's Park

Thai tapas restaurant on Salusbury Road that's genuinely one of the most family-friendly spots in the area. Dedicated kids' menu with mild Thai dishes, high chairs, colouring packs, and staff who actively welcome children rather than just tolerating them. The mezzanine level has extra space for buggies.

Why parents like it: Kids get proper food (not just beige freezer stuff), parents get excellent Thai — everyone wins. The lunch deal is great value.

Franco Manca

all ages£Queen's Park

Sourdough pizza that's fast, affordable, and perfect for families. Kids' pizza menu with smaller portions, high chairs, and the open kitchen keeps little ones entertained watching the dough being tossed. No fuss, no reservations needed, quick turnover — ideal when you need food NOW.

Why parents like it: Speed + quality + price = the holy trinity for family dining. In and out in 45 minutes if you need to be.

Queen's Park

Honest Burgers

all ages££Queen's Park

The Salusbury Road branch of this burger chain is reliably family-friendly. Little Honest kids' menu (burger, chicken strips, or fish fingers with chips and a drink), high chairs, colouring sheets, and enough space between tables for a buggy. The rosemary chips are legendary.

Why parents like it: Proper burgers for adults, proper kids' portions for children, zero pretension. Saturday lunch heaven.

West Hampstead

Banana Tree

all ages££West Hampstead

Pan-Asian restaurant on West End Lane with a dedicated "Little Monkeys" kids' menu — mild curries, noodles, and chicken satay. High chairs, colouring kits, and booth seating that works brilliantly for families. The flavours are interesting enough for adults without being scary for kids.

Why parents like it: Kids get to try something different beyond pizza and chips, but it's all mild and approachable. The lunch deal is excellent.

Kilburn

Little Bay

all ages££Kilburn

Kilburn institution with famously quirky decor — theatre-style booths, opera boxes, and velvet everywhere. Kids love the eccentricity. Children's menu (fish fingers, pasta, mini roasts), high chairs, plenty of buggy space, and staff who've been dealing with families for decades.

Why parents like it: The decor alone buys you 20 minutes of distraction. Great value, proper portions, and a proper Kilburn experience.

West Hampstead

Pizza Express

all ages££West Hampstead

The classic family chain — and for good reason. The Piccolo kids' menu (starter, main, dessert, and a Bambinoccino for £8.95) is genuinely good value. Dough balls are a rite of passage. High chairs, colouring packs, and the West End Lane branch has a relaxed, spacious layout.

Why parents like it: You know exactly what you're getting, the kids are always welcome, and the dough balls arrive before anyone gets hangry.

Cricklewood

Sarracino

all ages££Cricklewood

Family-run Italian on Cricklewood Broadway with a proper kids' pizza and pasta menu. Relaxed trattoria vibe where nobody's fussing about noise. Generous table spacing means room for buggies, and the staff are genuinely warm with children. The tiramisu is worth saving room for.

Why parents like it: Feels like a proper neighbourhood Italian — not a chain, not pretentious, just good food and a genuine welcome.

Restaurant
Hampstead

Rossopomodoro

all ages££Hampstead

Proper Neapolitan pizza on Flask Walk in the heart of Hampstead. Special kids' pizza menu with smaller Margheritas and Marinaras, colouring mats and crayons, and an open kitchen where kids can watch pizzas being made. High chairs and a genuinely warm welcome from the Italian staff.

Why parents like it: Excellent pizza in a gorgeous setting — feels like a treat without being stuffy. Pair with a Hampstead Heath walk.

West Hampstead

Bill's

all ages££West Hampstead

All-day restaurant on West End Lane that's basically designed for families. Excellent kids' menu (fish fingers, buttermilk chicken, pasta — plus a build-your-own sundae), high chairs, colouring packs, and a children-eat-free deal during school holidays. The brunch is particularly good.

Why parents like it: Goes from brunch to dinner seamlessly, the kids' menu is actually good, and the holiday free-meal deal is a genuine money-saver.

Kilburn / Staples Corner

Nando's

all ages£Kilburn

Say what you like — Nando's is the ultimate family fallback. The Nandinos kids' menu (£5.95 for a main, two sides, and a drink) is hard to beat on value. High chairs, quick service, no booking needed, and the Kilburn High Road branch is spacious. The frozen yogurt machine buys you an extra 10 minutes of peace.

Why parents like it: Fast, predictable, affordable, and every child on earth already knows what they want. The definition of low-stress family dining.
