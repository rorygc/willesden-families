West Hampstead

Sarracino

all ages££West Hampstead

Family-run Italian on Broadhurst Gardens with a proper kids' pizza and pasta menu. Relaxed trattoria vibe where nobody's fussing about noise. Generous table spacing means room for buggies, and the staff are genuinely warm with children. The tiramisu is worth saving room for.

Why parents like it: Feels like a proper neighbourhood Italian — not a chain, not pretentious, just good food and a genuine welcome.

Queen's Park

Pizza Pilgrims

all ages£Queen's Park

Neapolitan pizza on Salusbury Road. Open kitchen means free entertainment watching dough being tossed. High chairs, relaxed pace, and they don't blink at the mess kids make. The simple Margherita is one of the best-value meals in the area.

Why parents like it: Quick, excellent pizza at a fair price. Perfect post-park lunch with the playground just up the road.

Kilburn

Quartieri

all ages££Kilburn

Neapolitan pizza and pasta on Kilburn High Road from a team born and raised in Naples. Casual, energetic spot open daily from 11am til late. Proper thin-crust pizza, fresh pasta, and a relaxed attitude that makes it easy with kids in tow. Reservations available, delivery via Deliveroo and UberEats.

Why parents like it: Genuine Neapolitan cooking at reasonable prices, no stuffiness, and a menu that kids instinctively understand — pizza and pasta.

Willesden Green

Sanzio

all ages££Willesden Green

Long-standing neighbourhood Italian on Station Parade, Willesden Green. Traditional pizzas, pastas, and grill dishes in a warm, unpretentious setting. A proper local institution — the kind of place where families have been coming for years and the staff remember your order.

Why parents like it: Reliable, welcoming, and right by Willesden Green station. Perfect for an easy midweek dinner when nobody wants to cook.
