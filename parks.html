Swings, slides, duck ponds, and space to run.

Ten of the best parks and playgrounds across NW London — from the vastness of Hampstead Heath to the hidden gem of Mapesbury Dell. All with playgrounds, most with cafés, and every single one free.

Queen's Park

Queen's Park

all agesFreeQueen's Park

The crown jewel of the area. Fenced playground with separate toddler and junior areas, small zoo with farm animals, tennis courts, and a café. The playground has sandpits, climbing frames, and plenty of benches for parents. Gets busy on sunny weekends but big enough to absorb the crowds. Note: the paddling pool has been closed for several years — see our splash pads page for the campaign to reopen it.

Why parents like it: Playground, zoo, café, and tennis all in one walkable park. Pack a picnic and you've got a full day out for free. (And if you want water play, check our splash pads page.)

Dollis Hill

Gladstone Park

all agesFreeDollis Hill

Large hilltop park with a well-equipped playground, duck pond, walled garden, café, and sports facilities. The playground has a good range of equipment for different ages. The café does decent coffee and ice cream. Views across London from the top are excellent — great spot for kite-flying.

Why parents like it: The walled garden is a hidden gem — beautiful, peaceful, and toddlers love running along the paths. Bring bread for the ducks.

Willesden

Roundwood Park

all agesFreeWillesden

Victorian park with formal gardens, a café in the lodge, aviary, and a good playground. The playground was recently refurbished and has equipment for toddlers through to older kids. The café does homemade cakes and proper coffee. Lovely flower displays in summer.

Why parents like it: More relaxed than Queen's Park, rarely overwhelmingly busy. The aviary is a nice bonus — kids love watching the birds.

Hampstead

Hampstead Heath

all agesFreeHampstead

Not just a park — 790 acres of wilderness in London. Multiple playgrounds (the one near Parliament Hill is the biggest), swimming ponds (including a paddling pool for kids), kite-flying on Parliament Hill, and endless space to run. The Parliament Hill café is a life-saver. Combine with a visit to the Freemasons Arms or Spaniards Inn after.

Why parents like it: Kids can actually run wild here — proper exploring, climbing trees, rolling down hills. The scale means it never feels crowded even on bank holidays.

Maida Vale

Paddington Recreation Ground

all agesFreeMaida Vale

Massive green space with one of the best playgrounds in the area — fully fenced, huge range of equipment, separate toddler and junior areas with soft surfaces. Café on site, sports facilities, and plenty of benches. The playground is the standout feature — big enough that kids don't get bored after 20 minutes.

Why parents like it: The playground is the real draw — expansive, varied, and fully enclosed so you can actually relax while the kids play.

Kilburn

Kilburn Grange Park

all agesFreeKilburn

Neat community park with a recently upgraded playground, outdoor gym, and landscaped gardens. Not the biggest but perfectly formed for a morning or after-school play session. The playground has a good range of equipment and there are plenty of benches. No café but close to Kilburn High Road for supplies.

Why parents like it: Great local park for a quick play — no need to make a day of it. The playground equipment is modern and well-maintained.

Mapesbury / Willesden Green

Mapesbury Dell

under 8sFreeMapesbury

A true hidden gem — a tiny community-run park tucked away behind Willesden Green. Small but lovely playground, community garden, and a wonderfully peaceful atmosphere. The playground is best for under-8s. Volunteers keep it beautifully maintained. No café but the sense of discovery makes up for it.

Why parents like it: Feels like a secret garden. Perfect for a quiet morning play with little ones — rarely busy and the community garden is genuinely lovely.

Kensal Rise

Tiverton Green

under 8sFreeKensal Rise

Small neighbourhood park with a well-maintained playground, green space, and benches. Perfect for a quick play on the way to or from Kensal Rise. The playground has swings, slides, and climbing equipment suitable for toddlers through to about age 8. Close to Chamberlayne Road for a post-play coffee.

Why parents like it: Handy local spot — not worth a special trip but perfect if you live nearby or are in the area. Rarely crowded.

West Hampstead

Fortune Green

all agesFreeWest Hampstead

Pleasant green space with a playground and benches, popular with local families. The playground has basic equipment — swings, a slide, climbing frame — but it's well-kept and the surrounding green is good for ball games and picnics. Close to West Hampstead's cafés and shops.

Why parents like it: Solid local option — nothing flashy but reliable and well-located. Good before or after a West Hampstead coffee run.
