Family-friendly pubs in North West London

Beer gardens, Sunday roasts, and places where kids are genuinely welcome.

Eight tried-and-tested pubs across Queen's Park, Kensal Rise, West Hampstead, Kilburn, Hampstead, and Cricklewood — all with outdoor space, high chairs, and a proper family-friendly attitude.

Queen's Park

The Alice House

all ages ££ Queen's Park

Gastropub with a large beer garden at the back — one of the most family-friendly spots on Salusbury Road. High chairs, changing facilities, and a relaxed weekend vibe where nobody blinks at a toddler uprising. Good Sunday roasts.

Why parents like it: Best all-rounder for families in Queen's Park — big garden, great food, genuinely welcoming staff.

Queen's Park

The Salusbury

all ages £££ Queen's Park

Wine bar-meets-gastropub with a continental feel. Kids are genuinely welcome — not just tolerated. The deli counter and bakery make it an easy stop any time of day, and the outdoor tables on Salusbury Road are prime parent-watching territory.

Why parents like it: Perfect for a civilised lunch where you actually get to finish your meal while the kids colour.

West Hampstead

The Gallery

all ages ££ West Hampstead

Spacious gastropub on Broadhurst Gardens with a large beer garden that fills with families at weekends. The garden is the main draw — plenty of room for buggies and wandering toddlers. Solid pub food menu with kids' options.

Why parents like it: The garden is the star here — one of the biggest in the area and properly set up for families.

Kensal Rise / West Hampstead

The Black Lion

all ages ££ Kensal Rise

Beautifully renovated pub on West End Lane with a large beer garden. Dogs and kids equally welcome. The garden is a suntrap in summer and the menu covers everything from proper roasts to burgers. High chairs available.

Why parents like it: The garden is a proper suntrap — great for a lazy weekend afternoon with the family.

Kilburn

The Old Bell

all ages ££ Kilburn

Gastropub on Willesden Lane with a relaxed beer garden. One of those places where families, after-work drinks, and Sunday roasts all coexist without friction. Baby change facilities and high chairs.

Why parents like it: Solid local that handles the full spectrum — quiet weekday lunch to busy Sunday roast — without ever feeling like kids aren't welcome.

Hampstead

The Freemasons Arms

all ages ££ Hampstead

Right next to Hampstead Heath with a huge beer garden — the textbook post-Heath-walk family pub. The garden is enormous and very relaxed, with plenty of space for kids to run around. Menu covers pub classics, pizzas, and kids' portions.

Why parents like it: The ultimate family pub for a Heath day — massive garden, zero pretension, everyone's there for the same reason.

Pub
Hampstead

The Spaniards Inn

all ages £££ Hampstead

Historic 16th-century pub with one of the biggest and loveliest beer gardens in North London. Right on the edge of the Heath. Extremely family-friendly — the garden is practically a village green. Keats and Dickens drank here; now it's mostly parents with buggies.

Why parents like it: Worth the trip for the garden alone — feels like a country pub in the middle of London. Perfect summer destination.

Cricklewood

The Crown

all ages £ Cricklewood

Big pub on Cricklewood Broadway with a decent outdoor area and a properly family-friendly attitude. More of a traditional pub feel than the gastropubs — affordable, unpretentious, and the kind of place where nobody minds a bit of noise.

Why parents like it: Best value family pub in the area — no gastropub mark-up, just honest pub food in a welcoming space.
