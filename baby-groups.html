Toot Toot Stay and Play at Kings Hall

0–5£7 / £3Willesden

Drop-in stay and play for under-5s at Kings Hall Community Centre. A proper morning of toys, parachute games, bubbles, and singing with use of a garden. Hot drink and snacks included — rare at this price. £7 per session for 6 months+, £3 for babies 0–6 months. Runs Mondays and Fridays 9:30–11:15am during term time. Closed for summer break.

Why parents like it: Genuinely good value — the included hot drink and snacks mean you're not paying extra for a café. Parachute games, garden, and a warm community vibe. Perfect way to meet other local parents with young kids.

Willesden Green Library — Rhyme Time

0–5FreeWillesden Green

Weekly free rhyme time session at the Library at Willesden Green. Songs, nursery rhymes, and simple stories for babies and toddlers with parents or carers. Drop-in — no booking needed. The children's library area is excellent for staying afterwards to read and play.

Why parents like it: Free, drop-in, and you can combine it with library books and the café downstairs. The perfect cheap morning out.

Library Baby Bounce & Rhyme Time

0–2FreeQueen's Park / Kilburn

Queen's Park Library and Kilburn Library both run free weekly baby bounce and rhyme time sessions. Songs, bouncing rhymes, and sensory play for the youngest ones. Both libraries are buggy-accessible and have children's areas. Check individual library timetables for days and times.

Why parents like it: Free, local, and a great way to meet other new parents in the area. The librarians are brilliant with babies.

Baby Sensory

0–13 months££Various

The gold standard of baby classes. Each week has a different theme with lights, music, textures, puppets, bubbles, and signing. Classes run in Queen's Park, West Hampstead, and Kilburn. Book in advance — they fill up fast. The first class is usually a taster. Excellent for baby development and parent sanity.

Why parents like it: The production quality is genuinely impressive — it's as entertaining for parents as it is for babies. Worth every penny.

Monkey Music

3 months–4 years££Queen's Park

Award-winning music classes with age-specific groups: Rock 'n' Roll (3–12 months), Heigh-Ho (1–2 years), Jiggety-Jig (2–3 years), and Ding-Dong (3–4 years). Real instruments, singing, dancing, and a lovely gentle atmosphere. Classes in Queen's Park and West Hampstead.

Why parents like it: The progression through age groups means your child can do Monkey Music for years. Lovely teachers and proper musical education disguised as fun.

Hartbeeps

0–4 years££Various

Immersive music and sensory classes with a theatrical twist — each session is a mini production with props, puppets, light shows, and soundtracks. Classes are split by age: Baby Beeps (sitting to crawling), Happy House (toddlers), and Baby Bells (newborns). Fun, lively, and slightly bonkers in the best way.

Various NW London

NCT Bumps & Babies

0–18 monthsLowVarious

Weekly informal drop-in groups for expectant parents and parents with babies up to 18 months. Run by local NCT volunteers in community venues across NW London. A cup of tea, a biscuit, and a room full of people who get it. The perfect antidote to the isolation of early parenthood.

Why parents like it: No judgment, no agenda — just tea, chat, and other parents. Often the first place new parents make local friends.

Brent Family Hubs & Children's Centres

0–5FreeBrent

Brent's network of Family Hubs and Children's Centres run free stay-and-play sessions, baby groups, breastfeeding support, health visitor drop-ins, and parenting courses. Locations include Granville (Kilburn), Church End, and Curzon Crescent. Check the Brent website for timetables and the full hub list.

Why parents like it: Free, professional, and you can access health visitors and other support at the same time. The hub model means multiple services under one roof.

St Anne's & St Andrew's Toddler Group

0–4LowWillesden Green

Church hall stay-and-play run by volunteers. Toys, ride-ons, craft table, snack time, and singing at the end. Runs during term time. Small charge (£2–3) to cover refreshments. One of those community institutions that's been running for years — warm, welcoming, and properly local.

Why parents like it: Old-school community playgroup vibes. Cheap, friendly, and you'll see the same faces every week.

Little Critters Stay 'n' Play at Our Yard

0–5FreeBrent Cross

Free drop-in stay and play session for under-5s at Our Yard, the community hub at historic Clitterhouse Farm. A relaxed morning of toys, play, socialising, and fresh air in the garden when weather allows. Fridays during term time. The garden setting makes it feel less institutional than church hall groups.

Why parents like it: Free, outdoorsy, community-led, and genuinely lovely. The garden is a proper sun-trap in summer.

The Hearth — Toddler Mornings

0–4£Queen's Park

The Hearth on Chamberlayne Road runs informal toddler mornings with coffee, cake, and a relaxed space for little ones to play. Not a structured class — more of a warm, welcoming café session where babies and toddlers can potter while parents actually get to drink a hot coffee. Check their calendar for days.

Why parents like it: The coffee is genuinely good, the vibe is calm, and you don't feel like you're at a children's party. Perfect for a low-key morning.
