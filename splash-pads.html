Queen's Park

Queen's Park paddling pool — CLOSED

❌ Closed for yearsall agesQueen's Park

The paddling pool in Queen's Park has been closed for several years and remains out of action. It's one of the most glaring gaps in NW London's family infrastructure — a huge, purpose-built paddling pool sitting empty while families swelter in summer. The playground, zoo, and café are all still open and excellent, but the pool itself is dried up and fenced off.

🚨 This needs a campaign. Brent Council has been promising action for years. Every hot weekend parents ask each other "is the pool open yet?" and the answer is always no. A proper local campaign — petitions, councillor pressure, community action — could get this reopened. If you're up for organising something, this is the fight worth having.

North Kensington

Kensington Memorial Park paddling pool

0–10FreeLadbroke Grove

Large, well-maintained paddling pool in Kensington Memorial Park (St Mark's Road, W10). Open daily in summer months — check RBKC website for exact season dates. Good playground right next to the pool, plus toilets and plenty of green space for picnics. Local cafés on Golborne Road are a short walk away. Less crowded than Queen's Park on peak days.

Why parents like it: Big pool, good playground alongside, and usually less packed than the bigger-name parks. Bring snacks — the nearest cafés are a few minutes' walk.

North Kensington

Meanwhile Gardens Playhut paddling pool

0–6Free/£3Ladbroke Grove

Secure garden with a paddling pool at this eco-friendly community play centre in Meanwhile Gardens (opposite 27 Elkstone Rd, W10 5NT). Also has a sandpit, slide, and indoor/outdoor play equipment. Free for Kensington & Chelsea residents, £3 per family otherwise. Regular sessions during the week — email hayley@meanwhile-gardens.org.uk for the current timetable. Best for under-6s.

Why parents like it: Proper community gem — secure garden, eco-friendly, and the paddling pool is the perfect size for toddlers. Much more personal than a big council-run pool.

Maida Vale

Paddington Recreation Ground splash pad

all agesFreeMaida Vale

One of London's oldest and best-loved public sports grounds, with a dedicated splash pad/water play area open in summer. Also has a playground, tennis courts, athletics track, and a café. The splash pad has multiple jets and water features — more active and exciting than a traditional paddling pool. Good for toddlers and primary-age kids alike. Big lawns for picnics and drying off.

Why parents like it: The splash pad is more fun than a static pool — jets, sprays, and running through water. The café and proper toilets make it an easy half-day outing.

Bloomsbury

Coram's Fields splash pad & paddling pool

0–16FreeBloomsbury

Seven acres of central London space exclusively for children and their families — adults can only enter with a child. Splash pad with water jets plus a separate paddling pool, both open in summer. Also has playgrounds for different age groups, sandpits, a city farm with goats and chickens, a café, and sports pitches. The splash pad is particularly good — multiple jets, colourful and well-designed. Near Russell Square tube.

Why parents like it: The adults-must-have-a-child rule means the whole place feels safe and family-focused. Splash pad, paddling pool, AND a city farm. Worth the trip into town.

King's Cross

Granary Square fountains

all agesFreeKing's Cross

The famous dancing fountains at Granary Square — 1,080 individually-controlled water jets spread across four acres of public square. Kids absolutely love running through them on hot days. The fountains are choreographed in patterns (\"dancing\") and completely safe — no deep water, just jets shooting up from the ground. The whole area around Coal Drops Yard and Regent's Canal is packed with cafés, restaurants, and ice cream spots. King's Cross station is 2 minutes away.

Why parents like it: Pure joy. Kids sprint through the jets for hours, the surrounding square has steps/seating for parents, and there's endless food and drink on your doorstep. Bring swimsuits and a change of clothes.

Hyde Park

Diana Memorial Fountain

all agesFreeHyde Park

Circular granite water feature in Hyde Park designed as a memorial to Princess Diana. The oval ring has water flowing in two directions — one side is a gentle ripple (perfect for toddlers to paddle), the other is a faster, splashier flow that older kids love. Set in beautiful landscaping with grassy slopes around it, perfect for picnics. Near the Serpentine, the Lido café, and the Diana playground (a must-visit in its own right — pirate ship, sensory trail, teepees).

Why parents like it: Combines a London landmark with genuinely great water play. Pair it with the Diana playground and the Lido café for a perfect summer day. The gentle-flow side is brilliant for nervous toddlers.

Strand / Temple

Somerset House fountains

all agesFreeStrand

Fifty-five ground-level fountains dancing in the grand Georgian courtyard of Somerset House. In summer, children (and plenty of adults) run through them freely — it's actively encouraged. The courtyard is fully pedestrianised with steps and ledges for sitting. Excellent cafés, a gallery, and one of London's most beautiful buildings as the backdrop. Right on the Strand, minutes from Temple station. Combine with a walk along the South Bank.

Why parents like it: Stunning setting, proper cafés with good coffee, and a courtyard that turns into a giant water playground all summer. Very Instagram-friendly if that's your thing.

Bethnal Green

Victoria Park splash pool

all agesFreeBethnal Green

The biggest splash pool in London — more like a shallow water playground than a paddling pool. Multiple fountains, sprays, and water features spread across a large area in the park's eastern edge. Open daily in summer months, usually 10am–6pm. Victoria Park itself is one of London's finest — huge, beautiful, with playgrounds, cafés, a boating lake, and weekend food markets. A longer journey from NW London but a proper day out.

Why parents like it: The sheer scale — this is the biggest splash spot in London. Kids go absolutely wild for it. Pair with the park's playgrounds and food market for a full-day summer adventure.

Splash pad survival guide ☀️

Some things we've learned the hard way:

  • Bring two towels per child. One for drying off, one for sitting on. They will get soaked.
  • Swim nappies are essential for little ones — most splash pads require them.
  • Water shoes or sandals — fountains and splash pads can get slippery, and hot paving hurts bare feet.
  • Sunscreen before you arrive. Wet kids + sunscreen application = chaos. Do it at home.
  • Change of clothes — including for yourself. You will get splashed.
  • Check opening hours before you go. Most paddling pools and splash pads are seasonal (May–September) and some close for cleaning on certain days.
  • Arrive early on hot weekends. By 11am on a 28°C Saturday, the best spots are gone.
Best for toddlersKensington Memorial Park and Meanwhile Gardens — shallow, fenced, designed for little ones. (Queen's Park paddling pool is still closed.)
Best for big kidsGranary Square and Victoria Park — active jets, lots of running, maximum chaos.
Best all-rounderCoram's Fields — splash pad AND paddling pool AND city farm AND playgrounds, all in one place.
