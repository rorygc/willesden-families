Family day plans & ready-made itineraries

Pre-planned routes for when you can't be bothered to think.

Six tried-and-tested family day plans across NW London. Brunch → playground → pub. Rainy-day museum routes. Sunday morning coffee walks. Built by parents who've already done the trial and error.

☀️ The perfect Queen's Park Sunday morning

3–4 hours · walking · all ages · ££ · best on sunny weekends

  • 🥐
    Stop 1: Gails Bakery, Salusbury Road

    Grab coffees, pastries, and a babyccino. The window counter is great for people-watching while toddlers nibble croissants. Get there before 9:30 to avoid the queue.

  • 🌳
    Stop 2: Queen's Park playground

    Five-minute walk from Gails. Huge playground with separate toddler area, sandpit, and the best zip line in NW London. The duck pond is a hit with all ages — bring birdseed (not bread).

  • 🍺
    Stop 3: The Alice House, Chamberlayne Road

    15-minute walk or 5-minute drive. Proper Sunday roast, high chairs everywhere, colouring sheets, and a garden. Book ahead — Sunday lunch is popular. Kids' roast is £8.50.

🌧️ Rainy day: museums and pizza

4–5 hours · Jubilee line + walking · 3+ · mostly free · perfect when it's pouring

  • 🦕
    Stop 1: Natural History Museum

    Jubilee line to Green Park, then Piccadilly to South Kensington (~25 mins). Head straight to the dinosaurs, then the earthquake simulator. The Investigate centre (basement) lets kids handle real specimens. Free, but go early to beat crowds.

  • ☕
    Stop 2: Museum café or Exhibition Road

    NHM café is fine but busy. If the rain's eased, walk 3 mins to Exhibition Road for street food or Pret. The benches outside the V&A are covered — good spot for a packed lunch.

  • 🍕
    Stop 3: Pizza Pilgrims, Queen's Park

    Tube back to Queen's Park (~20 mins). Neapolitan pizza with an open kitchen (free entertainment), high chairs, kids welcome. Perfect wind-down meal after a big museum morning. In and out in 45 mins if you need to.

👶 Toddler morning: soft play and story time

2–3 hours · walking/driving · 0–4 · £ · any weather

  • 📚
    Stop 1: Willesden Green Library — children's floor

    Start with the free rhyme time or story session (check Brent Libraries timetable). The kids' floor has a brilliant open-plan layout with plenty of buggy space. Even without a session, the picture book area keeps toddlers happy.

  • 🍕
    Stop 2: Pizza Pilgrims, Salusbury Road

    Five-minute drive or 15-minute walk from the library. Quick Neapolitan pizza, high chairs, and an open kitchen kids love watching. Simple Margherita is excellent value. Ideal toddler lunch — in and out in 30 minutes.

🍂 Autumn walk: Kensal Rise to Queen's Park

2 hours · walking · all ages · £ · best Sep–Nov

  • ☕
    Stop 1: Coffee at Cable Co, Chamberlayne Road

    Pick up excellent coffee and a pastry from this Kensal Rise institution. They're lovely with kids and the window seats are prime buggy-parking territory.

  • 🍂
    Stop 2: Walk through College Road to the canal

    Wander down College Road (the tree-lined bit is gorgeous in autumn), cross over to the Grand Union Canal towpath. Collect conkers, spot narrowboats, feed ducks. About 25 minutes at toddler pace.

  • 🌳
    Stop 3: Queen's Park wildlife area

    Enter Queen's Park from the Harvist Road side. The wildlife area is tucked away and much quieter than the main playground. Perfect for nature-spotting and a peaceful end to the walk.

🎉 Saturday birthday-party survival plan

Party time + 2–3 hours post-party · driving · 3+ · ££

  • 🎂
    Stop 1: The party (Jump In! Cricklewood or your chosen venue)

    Drop-off parties are the dream, but if you're staying: bring headphones, a book, and treat the café coffee as a reward. The party host handles the kids — you're just moral support.

  • 🌳
    Stop 2: Bounce-off at Queen's Park playground

    Post-party sugar crash? The playground burns off the last of the cake energy. Aim for the sandpit if you're avoiding wipe-clean only clothes. The duck pond is a good calmer-down.

  • 🍺
    Stop 3: Parents' reward at The Salusbury

    You've earned a drink. The Salusbury on Salusbury Road has a good wine list, proper food, and a relaxed enough atmosphere that you can debrief the party chaos without glares from neighbouring tables.

🏙️ Central London big day out

Full day · Jubilee line + walking · 4+ · ££ · major expedition

  • 🔬
    Stop 1: Science Museum — Wonderlab

    Jubilee line to Green Park, Piccadilly to South Kensington. Book Wonderlab tickets in advance (worth the £10-ish for kids 7+). The Pattern Pod and The Garden (under-5s sensory space) are both free but need timed tickets on busy days.

  • 🍔
    Stop 2: Lunch at Byron or Honest Burgers, South Kensington

    Both are 5-minute walks from the museums, have kids' menus, high chairs, and fast service. Byron does a decent kids' deal. If the weather's good, Hyde Park is 10 minutes away for a picnic instead.

  • 🚌
    Stop 3: London Transport Museum, Covent Garden

    Tube to Covent Garden (or walk 30 mins through Hyde Park). Kids can climb aboard real buses and Tube trains. Under-18s free. The play zone on the top floor is excellent. Museum shop is expensive — set expectations.

  • 🍕
    Stop 4: Pizza Pilgrims, Queen's Park (homeward)

    Jubilee line back from Green Park to Queen's Park. You're all exhausted — Pizza Pilgrims is your no-booking-needed, fast-pizza, kids-welcome end to a big day.
